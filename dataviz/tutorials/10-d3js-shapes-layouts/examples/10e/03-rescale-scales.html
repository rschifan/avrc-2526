10e §4 — Rescale the scales

The right pattern for axes-aware charts: every zoom event calls transform.rescaleX(x) and rescaleY(y) to derive fresh scales, then reapplies them to both the dots and the axis generators. Tick labels and dot positions are computed from the same rescaled scale, so they stay aligned at every zoom level. Dot radii (in screen pixels) stay constant. Source tutorial →

Still imperfect: pan to the edge and dots leak outside the plot rectangle, over the axes and into the margins. The next example fixes that with a clip-path.

What this example shows

The canonical pattern for zooming a chart that has axes. Instead of moving SVG geometry, we move the scales: every zoom event derives fresh x and y scales from the transform, then redraws axes and repositions dots against those new scales. Visuals stay consistent because every mark is recomputed from the same source of truth.

  • transform.rescaleX(x) returns a new scale function. Its domain is the same as x; its range has been shifted and stretched by transform.x and transform.k. The original x is untouched, which is why you can reset to identity at any point. The same holds for rescaleY.
  • Two things to update in the handler, both using the rescaled scale: (1) the axes via axisG.call(d3.axisBottom(zx)) so tick positions and labels refresh, and (2) the data marks via cx = zx(d.gdpPercap) so they sit at the new pixel positions. Forget either one and they drift apart again.
  • Constant pixel radii: the dots' r attribute is never touched, so a 5-pixel dot stays 5 pixels at every zoom level. Stroke widths likewise. This is almost always what scatter readers expect.
  • Why this generalises: anything derived from the scales — gridlines, axis labels, hover tooltip positions, brush extents — refreshes for free when you reapply the rescaled scale. With the group-transform pattern you have to bookkeep which elements are inside the wrapper.
  • The remaining bug: dots are not constrained to the plot rectangle. Pan far enough and circles render over the axis ticks and into the margin — visually noisy and confusing. Fixed in §5 with a clipPath.
