11e §1 — Semantic zoom (zoom-aware labels)

d3.zoom on a wrapping <g>, with zoom-invariant sizing: every visual size attribute divides by k so strokes, radii and labels stay constant in screen pixels. Labels reveal only at k ≥ 1.5. Source tutorial →

What this example shows

One ingredient: a zoom behaviour and a rewrite of all visual sizes on every event. The result feels like "the chart zooms but the marks don't" — typography stays legible, dots stay readable, edges stay thin. This is the same group-transform zoom from 10e, with the extra step of counter-scaling every size attribute so it looks crisp at every zoom level.

  • Wrapper-group transform: zoomG.attr("transform", event.transform). SVG composes this with all descendants — every line and circle moves and scales as one. Borrowed straight from 10e §3.
  • The inv = 1 / k trick: with a 2× group scale, a normally 4-pixel circle becomes 8 pixels wide. Multiplying its r attribute by inv = 0.5 brings it back to 4 — visually constant across zoom levels. Same trick for stroke widths and font sizes.
  • Why not vector-effect: non-scaling-stroke: that attribute only handles strokes, not radii or label sizes. The 1 / k rewrite covers all three uniformly.
  • Label threshold: (k >= 1.5 || topLabelled.has(d.id)). At low zoom, only the 12 most-central characters are labelled (a curated set); zoom in and everyone gets a label. Same idea as semantic zoom in cartography: more detail emerges as you get closer.
  • No fit-to-canvas here: an auto-fit transform on the wrapper would fight the user's zoom transform. Instead the gentle forceX/forceY pull toward the canvas centre keeps the layout on screen during simulation. Pick one or the other — they don't compose.
  • Wheel to zoom, drag to pan. scaleExtent([0.3, 8]) lets the user zoom out to 30% and in to 8×.
