11f — Stage 3: Drag and hover

Two interactions on top of stage 2: d3.drag wired to fx / fy, and hover-to-highlight a focal node and its precomputed neighbours. Source tutorial →

What this stage adds (vs the previous stage)

Two well-isolated additions: the drag behaviour (start/drag/end → set/follow/clear fx / fy) and the hover-fade (precomputed neighbour set → opacity rewrite on enter / restore on leave). Both are the same patterns from 11c, no modifications.

  • Why these two together: drag lets the reader fix layout problems by hand (move an obscured node out of a cluster); hover lets them inspect connectivity without modifying state. They are complementary — the reader can arrange with drag and read with hover.
  • Drag handlers are 4 lines each — the brevity is exactly the point. D3 separates "what the gesture is" (its behaviour module) from "what to do when it fires" (your handler).
  • Hover is O(1) per event thanks to the precomputed neighbour Map — important on the 700-edge GoT graph.
